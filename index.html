I'm a Computer Systems Engineering student at Mehran University (MUET), currently in my third semester and figuring out how computers actually work — which mostly means asking "why isn't this compiling?" at 2 AM.

My interest in AI and machine learning started the way most good things do: by accident. I was trying to automate something tedious, stumbled into Python, and before I knew it I was reading papers about neural networks.

These days, I split my time between coursework, building desktop applications with Java Swing, and working through the Google AI Essentials curriculum. I enjoy programming that solves real problems for real people — like the electricity billing system I built after hearing one too many complaints about inaccurate utility bills in my area.

If you're working on something interesting in the AI/ML space — especially if it's practical and a little bit weird — I'd love to hear about it.

Featured Project  ·  Desktop Application

HESCO Electricity Bill & Power Management

Built after hearing one too many complaints about inaccurate utility bills. A full-featured desktop app that handles billing, tracks power usage, and flags potential theft by comparing transformer output against billed units.

The theft detection logic — modelling what "normal" power loss looks like versus suspicious discrepancies — was the most interesting challenge. Not deep learning yet, but rich in data validation and user role management.
